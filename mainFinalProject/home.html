Introduction

In order to answer our questions about the effects of gaming on life satisfaction, let's look at the biggest openly available dataset collected by Marian Sauter and Dejan Draschkow. You can access the original dataset here.

Although the dataset consists of over 13000 gamers from 109 countries of different age groups, I would like to focus on a certain subset of the data. As for data cleaning, I have removed responses which have values of "Not Applicable" so that I can do the relevant analyses. I have also filtered out the responses that are too unrealistic (e.g., 8000 hours of gameplay per week). Therefore, the main question of my analysis of this study would be:

Is there a negative impact of number of gaming hours per week on life satisfaction of League of Legends youth gamers?

First... What is League of Legends?

League of Legends (LoL), commonly referred to as League, is an online multiplayer battle arena game by RiotGames. Players gather to fight each other in teams, with games ranging from 15 minutes to 40 minutes on average. Since there is no limit to the number of games you can play, and there are plenty of characters (also known as champions) to pick from, one would find themselves moving from game to game without rest. The contents of the game might be the same, but the people you play with are always different, which result in different battle experiences. Therefore, one would not get tired of it easily and can play for hours.

I personally have an interest in this game and have also played for hours in a day before. (The video you saw at the start of this website is a video of my League of Legends gameplay!) Therefore, I thought that it would be interesting to dive into the effects of excessive gaming on life satisfaction.

Why do we still have low correlation?

Although the dataset claims to be the biggest dataset, it might not be the most accurate in showing a correlation between gaming and life satisfaction. Why? This is because there are many biases in the dataset, as well as the data collection methods.

Data Collection Method

Because of the sheer scale of the survey, it is very hard to ensure that there is random sampling, which allows for an equal representation of the population. Since Reddit seems to be a platform where many gamers over the world gather to discuss gameplay, the survey was posted on Reddit. Hence, we saw that most of the participants are users of Reddit, as compared to other platforms. Also, the participants are generally skewed towards males from North America and Europe. This results in a sampling bias.

Extraneous variables

We also cannot discount the fact that life satisfaction is correalted with a various number of other factors like social-economic status, race, education level, and more. Therefore, with there being lots of noise in the dataset, it is natural that a strong correlation cannot be found, unless we can control for the other variables.

Of course, there could still be the possibility of gaming hours not being very negatively correlated with life satisfaction. However, it is hard to come to a conclusion based on the current flawed data.

Concluding Thoughts

Even though the correlation that we have established between the average number of gaming hours and life satisfaction is weak, we still do see a small decline. This means that EXCESSIVE amounts of gaming might still be linked with life satisfaction. Therefore, it's always good to take note of your gaming hours and how gaming makes you feel.

Future research can focus on narrowing the scope to see if a stronger correlation between the two variables can be established. Perhaps a collaboration between researchers from over the world can be done. This would make it easier to get a more representative sample. Researchers should also attempt to control for possible extraneous variables.
